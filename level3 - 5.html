Relativity

 by Sarah Howe for Stephen Hawking




0When we wake up brushed by panic in the dark
our pupils grope for the shape of things we know.

1Photons loosed from slits like greyhounds at the track
reveal light’s doubleness in their cast shadows

2that stripe a dimmed lab’s wall—particles no more—
and with a wave bid all certainties goodbye.

3For what’s sure in a universe that dopplers
away like a siren’s midnight cry? They say

4a flash seen from on and off a hurtling train
will explain why time dilates like a perfect

5afternoon; predicts black holes where parallel lines
will meet, whose stark horizon even starlight,

6bent in its tracks, can’t resist. If we can think
this far, might not our eyes adjust to the dark?
